Climate

Deserts are extremely dry — very low precipitation, very hot days, and often cold nights. This creates a harsh but beautiful environment.

Plants

Desert plants are tough. Their adaptations let them survive with very little water.

Cactus: Water storage, spines, and waxy coating reduce water loss.

Desert Cacti Group: Having many cacti close helps shade the soil.

Succulent Cactus: Stores water in thick stems to survive drought.

Animals

Desert animals have special adaptations that help them live with very little water and extreme temperatures.

Fennec Fox: Big ears help release heat; nocturnal habits help avoid daytime heat.

Sand Lizard / Skink: Some skinks can “swim” through sand to escape predators.

Desert Reptile: Many reptiles have smooth scales to reduce water loss.

Geography

Deserts like the Sahara cover huge regions with dunes, rocky plateaus, and very little vegetation.

FAQs

  • What activities can you do? Sandboarding, camel rides, dune hiking, photography.
  • What clothes should you bring? Breathable clothing for day; warm layers for night.
  • Best time to visit? Fall or spring.
  • Any danger? Heat exhaustion, dehydration, sandstorms.
  • Must-see landmarks? Oases, dune fields, rock formations.
  • Threats? Climate change, desertification, human activity.
